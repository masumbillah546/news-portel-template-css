Arum farming on dried-up canal

Farmers in Faridpur Sadar upazila are opting for arum cultivation as it is more profitable than the other vegetable items. This year, a number of farmers in Sadipur area of the upazila are cultivating arum, locally called pani kochu, on the dried-up Mandartola canal, which is originated from the Padma river in Sadipur area and falls in the Kumar river in Bakhunda area. Cultivation of the nutritious vegetable sees gradual rise as it brings good profit for little production cost.
